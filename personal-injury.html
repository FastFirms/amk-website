What Is A Personal Injury Claim In Queensland?

A personal injury claim is a legal claim for compensation when someone else's negligence causes you injury — physical or psychological. In Queensland, claims are made against the at-fault party's insurer under legislation that depends on where and how you were hurt.

Road injuries run through the CTP scheme, work injuries through WorkCover, and injuries in public or private places through public liability insurance under the Civil Liability Act 2003. The same injury can be treated very differently depending on which scheme applies — which is why the first thing we do in a free case appraisal is identify the right pathway for your circumstances.

02

What Compensation Can You Claim For A Personal Injury?

Compensation can include medical and rehabilitation expenses, past and future loss of income and superannuation, the cost of care and assistance — including unpaid help from family — and general damages for pain and suffering.

For most injured people, future economic loss is the largest component: the income, career progression and working years the injury takes away. Courts assess this against your age, occupation and the medical evidence, which is why claims for the same injury can differ by hundreds of thousands of dollars. Building that evidence properly, early, is where experienced personal injury lawyers earn their keep.

03

How Does The Personal Injury Claims Process Work?

Most Queensland personal injury claims follow a pre-court procedure: notice forms to the insurer, exchange of evidence, independent medical examinations, and a compulsory settlement conference. The large majority of claims settle without a trial.

The process is designed to resolve claims, but it's also full of procedural traps and strict deadlines that insurers know intimately. We manage every step — lodgement, disclosure, medico-legal appointments and negotiation — and we prepare every claim as if it will go to trial, because that's what produces fair settlement offers.

04

What Are The Time Limits For Personal Injury Claims In Queensland?

The general limitation period is three years from the date of injury — but much shorter notice periods apply first: 9 months for CTP claims, 6 months for WorkCover, and prompt pre-court notices for public liability. Children generally have until their 21st birthday.

Time limits are the single most common way good claims are lost. Even if you're unsure whether you want to claim at all, getting advice early costs nothing and preserves every option. Evidence — CCTV, witness memories, incident records — degrades far faster than the legal deadlines.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions.

Do I have a personal injury claim?

If someone else's negligence caused your injury — a driver, an employer, a business, a property owner or a health provider — you likely have a claim. The fastest way to know is our free claim check: two minutes online, or a free consultation by phone, video or home visit around Queensland.

Can I claim for psychological injuries?

Yes. Psychological injuries — PTSD, depression, anxiety and adjustment disorders — are compensable in Queensland, whether they accompany a physical injury or stand alone. They require careful psychiatric evidence, which we arrange and fund as part of your claim.

What if my injury gets worse over time?

Claims are valued when your injuries have stabilised, precisely so deterioration is captured. This is also why accepting an early insurer offer is dangerous: once you settle, you generally cannot go back for more, even if your condition worsens.

Will making a claim affect the person I'm claiming against?

In almost all cases compensation is paid by an insurer — CTP, WorkCover or public liability — not by the individual personally. Claims against an at-fault friend, family member or employer are really claims against their insurance.

How long will my claim take?

Most claims resolve within 12 to 18 months once injuries stabilise. We'll give you a realistic timeframe at your free appraisal — and we never drag claims out, because we only get paid when you do.
