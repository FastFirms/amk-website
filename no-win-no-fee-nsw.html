How Does No Win, No Fee Work In NSW?

A No Win, No Fee arrangement (formally called a conditional costs agreement) means we only charge our legal fees if your claim succeeds. If your claim is unsuccessful, you pay us nothing. We also fund disbursements — medical reports, expert evidence, court filing fees — as we go, recovering them only from a successful outcome.

In NSW, conditional costs agreements are governed by the Legal Profession Uniform Law (NSW) No. 16a of 2014. The law requires that the agreement be in writing, disclose the basis for calculating costs, and explain any uplift component clearly. We provide a plain-English Cost Agreement before you sign anything, and we walk you through every term.

02

What Does "Genuine" No Win, No Fee Actually Mean?

Some firms advertise No Win, No Fee but charge interest on outlays, charge for initial consultations, or add uplift fees that significantly erode your settlement. At AMK: $0 upfront, $0 interest on disbursements we fund, and fees that are transparent and set out clearly in your Cost Agreement before we begin.

In NSW personal injury matters, the Legal Profession Uniform Law caps any uplift component of a conditional fee agreement at 25% of the professional costs. We explain exactly how our fees are calculated and what you will receive net of costs before you make any decision. A free second opinion on another firm's costs agreement is always available.

03

Are There Special Cost Rules For NSW CTP And Workers Compensation Claims?

Yes. NSW has specific regulated costs for both CTP and workers compensation matters. In icare statutory benefit disputes, legal costs are strictly regulated by SIRA and in many cases paid by the insurer — not deducted from your compensation. In CTP claims, MAIA 2017 provides for regulated cost scales in statutory benefit proceedings.

For common law damages claims (CTP and Work Injury Damages), costs are agreed or assessed at the end of the matter. The key point: in many NSW statutory benefit disputes, getting legal representation costs you nothing from your compensation at all — the insurer pays costs if you succeed. This is one of the most important and least-understood aspects of the NSW scheme.

04

What Happens To Legal Costs If You Win?

Legal costs in successful NSW personal injury claims are typically paid partly by the other side (party-party costs) and partly from your compensation (solicitor-client costs). Our Cost Agreement explains exactly how this works for your type of claim before you sign anything.

We are transparent about what you will receive net of all costs — not just the gross settlement figure. A settlement that looks large can look very different after costs are deducted. We model both figures for you so there are no surprises at settlement.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions.

What law governs No Win No Fee agreements in NSW?

The Legal Profession Uniform Law (NSW) No. 16a of 2014, applied in NSW through the Legal Profession Uniform Law Application Act 2014. Conditional costs agreements must be in writing and explain all costs clearly. Uplift fees in personal injury matters are capped at 25% of professional fees.

Do I pay anything if my claim is unsuccessful?

No. Under our conditional costs agreement, if your claim does not succeed you pay us nothing — no fees, no disbursements. That is what "No Win, No Fee" means. We assess claims carefully before accepting them, which is why the vast majority of claims we take on succeed.

What about disbursements — medical reports, experts?

We fund all disbursements upfront and recover them only from a successful outcome. We charge no interest on disbursements we fund. Everything is set out clearly in your Cost Agreement.

Are costs regulated for NSW CTP and icare claims?

Yes. SIRA regulates costs in CTP statutory benefit proceedings and icare disputes. In many statutory benefit matters, costs are paid by the insurer on success — not deducted from your compensation. This is one of the most important aspects of the NSW scheme that many claimants don't know.
