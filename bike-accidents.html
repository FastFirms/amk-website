01

Can A Cyclist Claim Compensation After Being Hit By A Car?

Yes. A cyclist injured by a driver's negligence claims against that driver's CTP insurance exactly like any other road user — covering injuries, lost income, treatment, care and pain and suffering.

This includes dooring incidents, left-hooks at intersections, and crashes where you took evasive action without contact. In Saul v Machalek, a game-fishing boat captain knocked off his bicycle by a negligently reversing vehicle was awarded $633,987 — and every contributory negligence argument against him was rejected. See more in our CTP recent cases.

02

What If A Pothole Or Road Hazard Caused Your Crash?

Crashes caused by potholes, debris, slippery surfaces, poor signage or dangerous road design may give rise to a public liability claim against the council or authority responsible for the road.

These claims turn on what the authority knew — or should have known — about the hazard, and whether its inspection regime was reasonable. Photograph the hazard and the location immediately: road defects get repaired fast once someone is hurt, and with them goes your best evidence.

03

Does Not Wearing A Helmet Affect Your Claim?

It can reduce — but rarely destroys — a claim. Queensland applies contributory negligence: if not wearing a helmet contributed to your head injuries, your damages may be reduced by a percentage. Injuries a helmet wouldn't have prevented are unaffected.

The same logic applies to lights, hi-vis and road positioning arguments insurers love to run. Don't accept the insurer's framing of fault without advice — these reductions are negotiated and contested, not automatic.

04

What Compensation Can An Injured Cyclist Claim?

Everything any injured road user can: medical and rehabilitation costs, past and future income loss, care and assistance, and general damages — plus your damaged bike and gear as part of the property claim.

For serious cycling injuries, future economic loss dominates. If you can no longer do physical work, ride to work, or maintain your pre-accident career, that loss is claimable — and it's where insurers fight hardest and proper evidence matters most.

05

What Should You Do After A Bike Accident?

Get medical attention, get the driver's details and rego, photograph the scene, your bike and your injuries, get witness names, and report to police. Then get legal advice before speaking to any insurer.

The CTP time limits apply to cyclists too: Accident Notification Form within 28 days, Notice of Accident Claim within 9 months — and just 3 months' notice if the driver can't be identified. Hit-and-runs against cyclists are depressingly common; the Nominal Defendant scheme exists for exactly that, but only if you move fast.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions.

Can I claim if the driver drove off and I don't know who they were?

Yes — through the Nominal Defendant, which stands in for the missing insurer. But notice must be given within 3 months, the claim can be barred after 9, and you must show proper search and inquiry for the vehicle. Report to police immediately and call us the same week.

I crashed avoiding a car that didn't hit me. Can I still claim?

Yes. Contact isn't required — if a driver's negligence forced you to take evasive action and you were injured, that's a claimable CTP event. Witness evidence becomes critical, so collect details at the scene.

Can I claim for my bike and gear?

Yes — your bicycle, helmet, clothing and accessories are claimable as property damage alongside your injury claim, usually against the at-fault driver's property insurer.

What if I was riding on a footpath or shared path?

Riding on footpaths is legal in Queensland for all ages. Crashes with vehicles at driveways and crossings are claimable, and hazards on shared paths may ground a claim against the council responsible.

Do e-bike riders have the same rights?

Compliant e-bike riders are treated as cyclists and claim through CTP the same way. Non-compliant or high-powered vehicles can complicate the picture — get advice on your specific setup.
