What Compensation Can You Claim After A Car Accident In Queensland?

After a car accident in Queensland, you can claim compensation for your medical and rehabilitation expenses, lost income (past and future), the cost of care and assistance, and pain and suffering — through the at-fault driver's CTP insurer.

The largest part of most claims isn't the medical bills — it's future economic loss: the income, superannuation and career progression the injury takes away from you. Care and assistance is also claimable even when it's provided unpaid by family and friends. How each of these is argued and evidenced makes an enormous difference to your final settlement, which is why two people with the same injury can receive very different amounts.

02

How Does A CTP Claim Work In Queensland?

Every registered Queensland vehicle carries Compulsory Third Party (CTP) insurance. When a driver causes an accident, injured people — drivers, passengers, pedestrians, cyclists and motorcyclists — claim against that driver's CTP insurer, not the driver personally.

The scheme is fault-based and overseen by Queensland's Motor Accident Insurance Commission under the Motor Accident Insurance Act 1994. You lodge a Notice of Accident Claim Form with the insurer, who investigates, funds reasonable treatment, and ultimately negotiates your damages. If you don't know the at-fault driver's registration or insurer, the Queensland Government can help you find those details.

Remember: the insurer's job is to minimise what it pays. Our team includes lawyers who used to defend these claims for insurers — we know the playbook, and we use it for you.

What Are The Time Limits For A Car Accident Claim In Queensland?

Three deadlines matter: an Accident Notification Form within 28 days (to access early treatment funding), a Notice of Accident Claim Form within 9 months of the accident or first symptoms — or within 1 month of seeing a lawyer — and court proceedings within 3 years.

If the at-fault vehicle was unregistered or can't be identified (a hit-and-run), your claim runs against the Nominal Defendant instead — and the deadlines are far tighter: notice within 3 months, and the claim can be permanently barred after 9. Missing a CTP deadline isn't always fatal, but you'll need a reasonable excuse or the court's permission, so the safest move is simple: get advice early. Our first consultation is free.

How Much Is A Car Accident Claim Worth?

There's no fixed figure — Queensland courts assess each claim on the injury's severity (its Injury Scale Value), your age, your earnings, and how the injury affects your ability to work and live. Recent Queensland awards we've analysed range from $5,000 to $2.49 million.

At the top end, a man seriously injured in a head-on collision was awarded $2,499,399 (Allen v O'Donnell). A cyclist knocked down by a reversing car received $633,987 (Saul v Machalek). At the other end, a self-represented claimant with a minor injury recovered just $5,000 — a cautionary tale about going it alone. 05

What Should You Do After A Car Accident?

Get safe and get medical attention first. Then: exchange details, photograph the scene and vehicles, get witness names, report to police if anyone was hurt, and see your GP — even for 'minor' symptoms — so your injuries are on the record from day one.

Two things to avoid: don't give a recorded statement to the at-fault driver's insurer without advice, and don't accept an early settlement offer before your injuries have stabilised. Insurers make early offers precisely because claims are usually worth more once the full picture is known.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions.

Can I claim if I was a passenger in the car accident?

Yes. Passengers can almost always claim, because a passenger is rarely at fault. You claim against the CTP insurer of whichever driver caused the crash — even if that driver was a family member or friend. Their insurer pays, not them personally.

Can I still claim if the accident was partly my fault?

Usually, yes. Queensland applies contributory negligence: your compensation is reduced by your share of the blame rather than wiped out. For example, if you were 25% at fault, you can still recover 75% of your assessed damages. Get advice before accepting the insurer's version of fault.

How long does a car accident compensation claim take?

Most CTP claims resolve in 12 to 18 months, once your injuries have stabilised enough to be properly assessed. Complex or seriously contested claims can take longer. Settling before your injuries stabilise risks under-compensation — which is why insurers often push early offers.

Will I have to go to court?

Almost certainly not. The vast majority of Queensland CTP claims settle at or before a compulsory conference, without a trial. Court is a last resort reserved for claims where the insurer won't make a fair offer — and having lawyers who are genuinely prepared to run a trial is what produces fair offers.

Do I need to report the accident to police?

Report the accident to police if anyone was injured, a vehicle was towed, or a driver failed to stop or exchange details. A police report number strengthens your claim, and for hit-and-run (Nominal Defendant) claims, prompt reporting and a proper search for the vehicle are effectively mandatory.

Is it worth getting a lawyer for a minor car accident injury?

Often, yes — 'minor' injuries like whiplash are routinely undervalued by insurers, and some develop into long-term problems. Because our first consultation is free and we act no win, no fee, it costs nothing to find out what your claim is actually worth before you accept anything.
