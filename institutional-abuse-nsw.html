Are There Time Limits For Institutional Abuse Claims In NSW?

In 2016, NSW removed limitation periods for civil claims arising from child sexual abuse through amendments to the Limitation Act 1969. This means there is no time bar on bringing a civil claim for abuse that occurred when you were under 18, regardless of when it happened or how long ago you became an adult.

For abuse occurring after you turned 18, general limitation periods apply (3 years from discoverability) but NSW courts also have a broad discretion to extend time. If you have been told you are "too late" to claim, that advice may be wrong — get a second opinion from a lawyer with specific institutional abuse experience.

02

What Is The National Redress Scheme And Should You Use It?

The National Redress Scheme provides a payment (up to $150,000), counselling and a direct personal response from the institution involved. It is available for survivors of institutional child sexual abuse at institutions that have joined the scheme. It is quicker and simpler than civil litigation — but the payments are capped and you waive your right to sue the institution.

Whether to apply for Redress or pursue a civil claim depends on your specific circumstances — the severity of the abuse, the strength of the evidence, and what outcome matters most to you. In many cases, civil litigation is worth substantially more than Redress. We advise on both options clearly so you can make an informed choice. There is no obligation to proceed with anything after our initial consultation.

03

Who Can Be Held Liable For Institutional Abuse In NSW?

Under the Civil Liability Act 2002 (NSW) and the common law, institutions — schools, churches, sporting clubs, government departments, hospitals, residential care facilities — can be held vicariously liable for abuse committed by their employees or agents, and directly liable for failures to properly select, supervise or manage staff.

The NSW Child Safe Standards and institutional duty of care obligations mean that evidence of inadequate supervision, known risks that were ignored, or a culture of secrecy around complaints can all strengthen a claim. We identify the proper defendant, locate insurance coverage, and build the evidentiary case with appropriate experts in trauma and psychology.

We move at your pace, communicate however you are most comfortable — phone, video, home visits across NSW, or in writing — and everything you tell us stays confidential. If you are not ready to make a decision today, that is fine. Your first conversation with us carries no obligation whatsoever.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions.

Is it too late to claim for abuse that happened decades ago?

For child sexual abuse in NSW, there is no time limit under the Limitation Act 1969 (NSW) as amended in 2016. For adult abuse, time limits may apply but courts have wide discretion to extend them. Don't assume you are too late — get advice.

Should I apply for the National Redress Scheme or sue?

Both options have merits. Redress is simpler and faster, with payments up to $150,000, but you waive your right to sue. Civil litigation can be worth substantially more for severe abuse. We advise on both options at your free consultation so you can choose what is right for you.

Will I have to go to court or confront the abuser?

Almost certainly not. Most institutional abuse claims in NSW resolve through confidential negotiation or mediation. You will never be required to confront an abuser, and nothing proceeds without your instructions.

What if the institution no longer exists?

We investigate whether successor organisations, government bodies or insurers carry liability. Many historic institutions that have closed still have accessible insurance coverage. Don't assume a claim is impossible because the original institution is gone.
